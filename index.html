What success looks like in week one
  • Logic Anki deck started with 10 cards minimum
  • LPL Ch. 1–2 read with retrieval notes written after, not during
  • Sagan Ch. 1–3 read, one Claim Check drafted on any health or science news story
  • Khan algebra diagnostic attempted and gaps identified honestly
  • 20 gesture sketches done across the week
  • French happened at least 4 days
That is a complete, successful first week. Nothing more is required.
What you're describing — talking too fast, saying things you immediately regret, thinking about yourself in groups — is not a personality flaw. It is what self-consciousness looks like from the inside. The fix is not becoming someone else. It is learning to redirect attention outward rather than inward, consistently, until it becomes automatic.
Daily practices
Morning intention (30 seconds)
Pick one word that describes who you want to be today. Not what you want to accomplish. Who you want to be. Grounded. Curious. Present. Patient. Carry it.
The one conversation practice
In one conversation today, say something and do not qualify it. Make the statement. Stop. Do not add "I don't know" or "if that makes sense." Let it sit. The silence after a considered statement is weight, not awkwardness.
The outward redirect
The moment you notice you are watching yourself from the outside, ask: what is genuinely interesting about this person right now? Find it. Your self-consciousness disappears when your attention is fully on someone else.
The weekly reach-out (Sunday)
One message to one person you have been meaning to contact. "I've been bad at staying in touch and I'm trying to change that. How are you?" is enough. Most people who like you are not keeping score.
Evening reflection (2 minutes, inside journal block)
Three questions: What did I do well today in how I showed up to other people? What didn't reflect who I want to be? One specific thing I will do differently tomorrow.
On speaking — slowing down
The physical habits
  • Slow down by 20% — deliberate pace reads as considered, not slow
  • Comfortable eye contact — genuine interest, not performed attentiveness
  • Ask one good question instead of several small ones — then listen to the full answer
  • Have positions. "Whatever you think" is an absence, not a position
Stop explaining yourself
  • "I don't drink tonight" needs no explanation
  • "I prefer to head home" needs no justification
  • "I'm not available then" is a complete sentence
  • The instinct to justify is the instinct to seek approval. Let your choices stand
The difference between "sorry, I'm still working on that" and "I have that for you right here" is almost never talent. It is the gap between doing work in your head versus doing it on paper early.
The anticipation habit
  • At the end of each workday, ask: what will someone need from me tomorrow that I could prepare tonight?
  • When you finish a task, ask: what is the logical next question someone will have? Answer it before they ask
  • Before any client-facing moment, write down the three things they are most likely to ask. Have the answers ready
The communication habits
  • When you send work, say what it is and what you want the recipient to do in the first sentence
  • Never send a problem without at least one proposed solution
  • Confirm your understanding of a task before you start it, not halfway through
  • If something will be late, say so 24 hours before it is due — not after
The meeting habit
Before every meeting, pick one thing you will contribute — one observation, one question, one piece of information you bring that others don't have. Say it. Then stop talking and listen fully.
The generous colleague habit
Once a week, do something that makes a colleague's job easier without being asked. Generosity at work builds reputation faster than any individual piece of work.
The end-of-day log (5 min, inside closing shift)
Three lines: what I completed today, what is outstanding and when it will be done, one thing I will do tomorrow before anyone has to ask me.
When you don't know something
  • "I don't have that in front of me right now — I'll get it to you by end of day" is professional and trustworthy
  • "I'm not sure but let me find out" followed by actually finding out is the entire job
  • Guessing to avoid looking uninformed is the only thing that actually undermines credibility
  • Saying "I'll confirm that" is a power move when you deliver

Recovery and non-negotiables

  • Prep food the night before on work days — the closing shift is when this happens
  • Weekly weigh-in Saturday morning, before eating, after bathroom — not more often
  • Rest days do not exist but recovery workouts do — 20 min of intentional movement counts
